In the rapidly growing field of e-commerce, understanding customer purchase behavior is essential for improving sales
and user experience. Businesses need to identify whether a user is likely to make a purchase based on their browsing activity,
such as time spent on the website, number of clicks, and product views. However, due to the large volume and complexity of user
data, it becomes difficult to manually analyze and predict customer decisions.

This project aims to solve this problem by developing a machine learning classification system that predicts whether a customer
will purchase a product or not, further more it will compare similar products that the customer is looking for and categories it
according to different factors such as(cost, quality, number of people that has bought it, reviews of the product, etc), and
make suggestions of products and items to the user.  By analysing historical browsing behaviour data, the system can assist
businesses in making data-driven decisions, improving targeted marketing strategies, and enhancing customer engagement. 
This leads to increased conversion rates and better personalization in online shopping platforms.
